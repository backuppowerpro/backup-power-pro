### 1. GOOGLE LOCAL SERVICES ADS (LSAs) — GRADE: A+

**What it is:** Pay-per-lead ads appearing above all other Google results — above paid PPC ads, above the map pack, above organic listings — with a "Google Guaranteed" or "Google Verified" badge. You only pay when a customer calls or messages directly through the ad.

**2025–2026 Benchmark Data:**
- CPL: $35–$70 for electrical contractors (HVAC runs $45–$85; plumbing $40–$75)
- Average CPL rising ~20% annually across home services; electrical up 23% in 2024
- Close rate: 40–50% (highest of any paid channel because these are active searchers)
- CPA: $70–$175
- A $75 LSA lead converting at 50% = $150 CPA vs. a $50 Thumbtack lead at 15% = $333 CPA

**Monthly budget to see volume:** $500–$1,500

**Time to first results:** 1–2 weeks after approval (background check + license verification takes 1–3 weeks)

**Effort level:** 2/5 — Set up profile thoroughly, then respond to leads within 60 seconds. The algorithm rewards fast response with lower costs and better placement.

**Scalability:** High — increase budget, expand to more zip codes, eventually more markets (when NC license is obtained)

**Why it is the #1 channel for BPP:**
- Pay-per-lead model means zero wasted spend on tire-kickers who never call
- Google Guaranteed badge builds instant trust before the customer even dials — critical when a homeowner is inviting someone into their electrical panel
- "Generator installation Greenville SC" is extremely high-intent; that person wants to book, not browse
- LSAs appear above all other Google real estate including paid PPC and the map pack
- Invalid leads (wrong number, outside service area) are automatically credited back

**SC/Southeast-specific notes:**
- Greenville-Spartanburg is market #59 — smaller than Atlanta or Charlotte, meaning CPL will likely land at the lower end of the $35–$50 range rather than the $65–$70 ceiling
- Competition in the LSA category for generator-specific installation is sparse; most competitors are general electricians who have not yet optimized their LSA profiles
- "Generator installation" as a specific sub-category has far less bid competition than "electrician near me"

**Monthly spend vs. revenue model:**
- $500/mo → 8–12 leads → 3–5 jobs → $4,800–$8,000 revenue (ROAS: 9:1–16:1)
- $1,000/mo → 15–22 leads → 6–9 jobs → $9,600–$14,400 revenue (ROAS: 9:1–14:1)
- $1,500/mo → 22–35 leads → 9–15 jobs → $14,400–$24,000 revenue (ROAS: 9:1–16:1)

**Action step:** Apply at ads.google.com/localservices. Requires background check, electrical license verification, and active insurance certificate. Collect at least 5 Google reviews before activating — the ranking algorithm heavily weights reviews.

### 2. GOOGLE BUSINESS PROFILE / LOCAL SEO — GRADE: A+

**What it is:** Free Google listing that populates the "Map Pack" (top 3 businesses shown on a map before organic results). More than half of Google searches in 2025 end without a website click — the GBP is the destination.

**2025 Benchmark Data:**
- CPL at maturity (12+ months of optimization): $10–$30 — essentially free qualified traffic
- Close rate: 35–45% (searching "near me" signals ready-to-hire intent)
- CPA at maturity: $25–$85
- Monthly cost: $0 (DIY) to $300 (professional local SEO management)

**Time to first results:** 3–6 months for initial map pack ranking; ongoing improvements compound over years

**Effort level:** 3/5 — Initial setup is intensive (full profile completion, citation building, schema markup). Ongoing maintenance is light (weekly posts, photo uploads, review responses).

**Scalability:** Medium — primary GBP ranks in one area; additional service area pages on the website can capture surrounding cities

**Key 2025 data points:**
- Over 97% of homeowners use Google to find local home services
- 75% of local searchers visit a business within 24 hours
- 28% of local searches result in a purchase
- Businesses in the Map Pack see 300–500% more traffic than page-2 organic results
- A well-optimized profile drives: 48% website clicks, 21% phone calls, 9% direction requests
- Google's AI Overviews now pull sentences directly from customer reviews — review content is a ranking signal

**BPP optimization priority list:**
1. Complete every single GBP field: service areas, all 20+ service types, hours, Q&A, products
2. Use "ElectricalContractor" business category schema on website (not generic "LocalBusiness")
3. Weekly GBP posts with before/after project photos, geo-tagged if possible
4. Build 30+ directory citations with exact-match NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across Yelp, BBB, Angi, etc.
5. Create city-specific landing pages for each of the 8 primary service cities
6. Review velocity: target 25 → 50 → 100 Google reviews; ask for specific keyword mentions
7. Add 5–10 job photos monthly; before/after photos of panel + inlet box + cord

**SC/Southeast notes:**
- Greenville has a growing digital economy but less established local SEO competition than Charlotte or Atlanta
- "Generator installation Greenville SC" and "transfer switch installation Simpsonville SC" have low keyword difficulty — capturing page 1 is achievable within 6 months
- Most direct competitors (general electricians) have incomplete GBP profiles and few reviews — the gap is wide open

**Monthly spend vs. revenue:**
- $0/mo (DIY only): 6–10 months to rank → 5–15 organic leads/month at maturity → $8,000–$24,000 revenue with $0 ongoing cost
- $300/mo (managed SEO): Faster ranking through citation building and content → 4–8 leads/month beginning at month 4–5

### 3. CUSTOMER REFERRAL PROGRAM ($75 DOUBLE-SIDED) — GRADE: A

**What it is:** Paying existing customers $75 (Visa gift card or check) for every referral who books. The referred customer receives $75 off their installation. The "Neighbor Bonus" program BPP has already designed.

**2025–2026 Benchmark Data:**
- Total incentive cost per booked referral: $75–$150
- Close rate: 70–80% (referred leads arrive with trust pre-built by someone they know)
- CPA: $95–$215 (incentive cost ÷ close rate)
- Monthly budget: $150–$300 in payouts at low volume; scales automatically as customer base grows

**Time to first results:** 1 month after first installations (need completed customers to refer)

**Effort level:** 2/5 — System setup is a one-time effort; execution happens at the end of every job

**Scalability:** High — scales automatically with the customer base; every job is a potential referral node

**Why this channel is exceptional for BPP:**
- Referred customers close at 60% vs. 15% for non-referred prospects (standard referral marketing data)
- Referred customers have 25% higher profit margins (Wharton study) — they trust the service from the start
- Double-sided programs generate 2.3x more shares and 1.8x higher conversion rates than single-sided alternatives
- You pay $150 total in incentives but collect $1,450 net revenue ($1,600 job minus $75 discount) = 867% ROI on incentive spend
- Zero wasted spend — you only pay when a job books and completes
- The timing is weaponized: ask at peak satisfaction moment (walkthrough complete, lights on, generator running)

**The referral math:**
- 20 completed jobs × 25% referral participation = 5 referrals/month
- 5 referrals × 75% close rate = 3–4 additional jobs/month
- 3 jobs × $1,450 net = $4,350 additional revenue from $450 in incentive costs
- That is a 9.7:1 return on incentive spend, from customers who found you for free

**Execution protocol:**
- NFC tap cards for Google reviews: hand to customer at walkthrough ("tap this to leave a quick review")
- Text within 24 hours of job completion: "Hey [name], it was great working with you! Know any neighbors who also own a generator and would love the same setup?"
- Leave 3 business cards + yard sign (with permission) at every job
- Include printed referral program card in the job completion packet
- Ask in person while the lights are on: peak-end rule says this is the highest-satisfaction moment

### 4. META / FACEBOOK ADS — GRADE: A

**What it is:** Paid advertising on Facebook and Instagram. Unlike Google (catching existing demand), Meta creates demand by interrupting people while they scroll through their feeds.

**2025–2026 Benchmark Data:**
- Average CPL across all industries: $27.66 (up 21% year-over-year)
- Home services CPL range: $30–$60 (some BPP-specific campaigns may run $40–$80 for niche offer)
- Google average CPL: $70.11 — Meta is significantly more cost-efficient at the CPL level
- Home improvement conversion rate: 5.22% (declining — down 36% from prior year due to market saturation)
- Close rate for BPP: 20–30% (colder leads than Google; need follow-up nurture sequence)
- CPA: $100–$300

**Monthly budget for volume:** $500–$2,000

**Time to first results:** 1–2 weeks (algorithm needs 2–4 weeks to fully optimize)

**Effort level:** 3/5 — Creative production is the ongoing cost; requires disciplined A/B testing and creative refresh every 4–6 weeks

**Scalability:** High — the Greenville metro has sufficient homeowner population to scale to $3,000–$5,000/month before hitting saturation

**Why Meta works for BPP specifically:**
- You can target homeowners aged 35–65 by zip code in Upstate SC with precision impossible on Google
- Video of a storm + dark house + "your generator is sitting in the garage, useless" triggers loss aversion — the most powerful emotional motivator (2x more effective than gain framing)
- The price anchor ($1,400–$2,000 vs. $15K standby) communicates instantly in a 15-second video
- BPP's sunk-cost positioning ("you already own the generator") is a uniquely compelling hook that feels like revealing a solution rather than selling a product
- Facebook's demographic skew (35–65 homeowners) matches BPP's target almost exactly in Upstate SC

**2025 watch-outs:**
- Home improvement CVR declining fastest of any category — creative fatigue is a real problem; plan 4–8 new creatives per month
- iOS privacy changes mean some attribution is modeled, not exact; trust blended data not single-click attribution
- Algorithm heavily rewards quality video; static image ads underperform significantly

**Monthly spend vs. revenue:**
- $500/mo → 8–15 leads → 2–4 jobs → $3,200–$6,400 revenue (ROAS: 6:1–12:1)
- $1,000/mo → 16–28 leads → 4–8 jobs → $6,400–$12,800 revenue (ROAS: 6:1–12:1)
- $2,000/mo → 30–50 leads → 8–15 jobs → $12,800–$24,000 revenue (ROAS: 6:1–12:1)

**Strategic note:** LSAs should be activated and proven BEFORE adding PPC. LSA ads appear above PPC ads in search results and charge per lead (not per click). Once LSA hits capacity, add PPC to capture additional search volume.

### 6. DOOR HANGERS — GRADE: A-

**What it is:** Printed promotional cards hung on neighbors' door knobs, deployed in clusters around active job sites and target neighborhoods.

**2025 Benchmark Data:**
- Cost per hanger: $0.35–$0.65 (printing + distribution)
- Response rate: 1–3% standard; 3–5% with compelling offer + hyper-local targeting
- CPL: $18–$30 — among the lowest of any marketing channel
- Close rate: 20–30%
- CPA: $60–$150

**Time to first results:** 2–4 weeks per campaign

**Effort level:** 3/5 — Printing logistics, distribution planning, hiring people to hang them at scale

**Scalability:** Medium — labor-intensive; best deployed by Key or the day's sub immediately after each install

**Why door hangers are exceptional for BPP:**
- The cloverleaf technique: hang 25–50 doors in every direction from each completed job site
- "Your neighbor on [street] just had their generator connected — they paid $1,400 instead of $15,000" is the most targeted message possible
- Neighbors are pre-qualified by definition (same neighborhood = same demographics, same home age, same storm experience)
- A neighbor who saw your truck in the driveway AND received a door hanger has experienced two touchpoints — significantly higher conversion
- Combine with a tracking phone number to measure ROI per deployment batch

**Door hanger math (conservative scenario):**
- 500 hangers × $0.50 all-in = $250 total cost
- 2% response = 10 calls
- 30% close rate = 3 jobs
- 3 jobs × $1,600 = $4,800 revenue on $250 spend = 19:1 ROAS

**Execution tip:** Pre-print 500 hangers at a time. Keep a stack in the truck. Deploy same-day as every install — the neighbor sees the truck, then finds the hanger on their door that evening.

### 7. EDDM (EVERY DOOR DIRECT MAIL) — GRADE: B+

- Close rate: 20–30%
- CPA: $100–$375

**Time to first results:** 3–5 weeks (print lead time + mail delivery + response window)

**Effort level:** 2/5 — Design once, select routes using USPS online tool, drop at post office, done

**Scalability:** High — can systematically mail the entire Greenville metro over time, route by route

**Best use case for BPP:**

- Include a QR code linking to get-quote.html for digital tracking
- Use personalization where possible: "Homeowners on [route/neighborhood name] are getting storm-ready"

**SC/Southeast notes:**
- Upstate SC has strong homeowner-occupancy rates — excellent for EDDM (renters rarely own generators)
- Post-Hurricane Helene awareness means storm preparedness messaging resonates strongly in 2025–2026
- Suburban sprawl around Greenville = dense single-family home routes with minimal apartment dilution

## CHANNEL INTERACTION EFFECTS (OFTEN IGNORED)

The channels above work better in combination than in isolation. Key interaction effects:

**LSA + GBP reviews:** Every review collected for LSA improves GBP ranking, which improves organic Map Pack position, which generates free leads that supplement paid LSA leads. These two channels are symbiotic.

**Vehicle wrap + door hangers + yard signs:** A homeowner sees the truck in a neighbor's driveway, finds a door hanger that evening, and sees a yard sign at the intersection the next morning. Three touchpoints from one installation. Close rate for someone who experienced all three is dramatically higher than for a single-channel cold lead.

**Meta Ads + referral program:** Meta brings in a new customer. That customer refers their neighbor. The neighbor refers their friend. One Meta lead can spawn a 3–5 person referral chain over 18 months — but only if the referral system is active from the first job.

**EDDM + storm surge:** Pre-storm EDDM mailing + storm surge ad activation creates an omnichannel moment where a homeowner receives a physical piece of mail AND sees digital ads within the same week. Response rate is 28–63% higher for campaigns that combine physical + digital vs. either alone.

**HOA partnership + GBP:** A single job in an HOA community generates a cluster of Google reviews from that neighborhood (if asked), boosting GBP relevance for that zip code and improving map pack rankings locally — which generates more leads in that same neighborhood organically.

## KEY STRATEGIC TAKEAWAYS

**1. Speed vs. sustainability:** LSA and Meta give you leads next week. GBP and SEO give you free leads forever starting in 6–12 months. Both are essential. Run them simultaneously from the start.

**2. The referral flywheel is the most powerful compounding asset:** Every completed job is a marketing asset. One satisfied customer in a neighborhood of similar homeowners creates exponential reach through referral, Nextdoor, Facebook groups, and yard signs. Systematize the referral ask from the very first job — the cost of not doing it is enormous.

**3. Never depend on rented platforms:** Angi, HomeAdvisor, and Thumbtack own the customer relationship — you're renting access to their database. LSA, Meta, and your own website own the customer relationship with you. Build owned channels first, rented channels as supplements.

**4. Storm season is the Super Bowl:** The April–October storm season is when generator connection demand spikes dramatically. All marketing systems need to be running and tested before April. Pre-build storm surge campaigns in March. Pre-load paused ad campaigns and just hit "activate" when NOAA issues a watch.

**5. Capacity is the binding constraint, not leads:** At $2,000/month in marketing, you will likely generate more leads than one person can install. Building the subcontractor network is not optional — it is the most important growth lever in the business. Marketing can only be scaled as fast as installation capacity allows.

**6. The $1,600 ticket justifies premium channels:** A $1,600 average job means you can spend up to $160 to acquire a customer (10% of ticket value) and still maintain strong margins. Many channels with CPAs of $100–$150 that appear expensive are actually quite profitable at your average ticket size. Don't optimize for cheapest CPL — optimize for CPA relative to margin.

**7. The niche is an advantage:** "Generator installation" is a far less competitive keyword category than "electrician." Your specificity allows you to win at far lower CPLs than a general electrical contractor. Maintain that niche positioning in all marketing — never dilute it with general electrical services messaging.
