What We're Installing

Backup Power Pro installs a generator inlet box on the exterior of your home and an interlock kit on your electrical panel. Together, these let your portable generator power your home's circuits safely and legally — with a full permit and inspection.

How the Interlock Protects You

The interlock kit is a physical sliding mechanism installed directly on your panel — not an electronic device.
It makes it mechanically impossible for both your main breaker and generator breaker to be on at the same time — preventing backfeed to the utility grid.
You cannot accidentally backfeed. The interlock physically won't allow it — no software, no settings, no user error can cause it.

Never tamper with, remove, or bypass the interlock kit. It is the core safety device in your system. It is sealed and should only be serviced by a licensed electrician. If it is ever damaged, call us immediately.

How to Use Your System

Place the generator outdoors only — at least 20 feet from any door, window, or vent. Never run it in a garage, even with the door open.
Start the generator before connecting. Let it warm up, then connect the power cord to the inlet box.
At the panel: Turn off your main breaker first, slide the interlock to expose the generator breaker, then turn it on. This is the correct sequence.
To restore utility power: Turn off the generator breaker, slide the interlock back, turn on your main breaker, then shut down the generator.

Never Do These

Never run the generator indoors or in any enclosed space — including garages with the door open. Carbon monoxide is odorless and fatal.
Never refuel while running. Shut down the generator and let it cool at least 2 minutes before adding fuel.
Never overload the generator. Stay within your generator's rated wattage. We'll review your load capacity during your walkthrough.
Never use the inlet box in wet conditions without the weatherproof cover closed. The outlet cover must be latched when not in use.
Never skip the shutdown sequence. Always disconnect at the panel before disconnecting the cord, and shut down the generator before removing the cord from the inlet.

If you smell gas, see sparks, or suspect carbon monoxide: Get everyone out of the home immediately and call 911. Do not re-enter until cleared by emergency services.

Maintenance & Your Warranty

Run your generator under load at least once a month — 30 minutes minimum. This keeps the engine ready and prevents fuel gelling.
Inspect the inlet box and cord annually for damage, corrosion, or worn insulation. If in doubt, call us before using the system.
Your installation comes with a workmanship warranty. Warranty is void if any component is modified, removed, or bypassed by an unlicensed party.
