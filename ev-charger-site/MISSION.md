# MISSION

## Objective
Build a profitable EV charger affiliate website from zero. Target $2,000/month revenue within 6 months.

## Constraints
- Budget: $0 (organic growth + free traffic sources only to start)
- Human intervention: Minimal. Owner handles account signups and domain purchase only.
- Revenue model: Affiliate commissions (Amazon Associates 3-4%, direct brand programs 8-15%)
- Hosting: GitHub Pages (free)

## Strategy (Agent-Directed)
The agent decides:
- Which keywords to target
- What articles to write and in what order
- When and where to distribute content
- How to build backlinks and authority
- When to pivot strategy based on results

## Revenue Channels (Priority Order)
1. Direct affiliate programs (ChargePoint, Wallbox, Emporia, Grizzl-E) — 8-15% commission
2. Amazon Associates — 3-4% commission on full cart
3. Display ads (once traffic hits 10K sessions/month) — Mediavine/AdThrive
4. Email list → product recommendations
5. Paid ads to high-converting content (once profitable organically)

## Traffic Channels (Priority Order)
1. Google organic SEO (long-term, highest volume)
2. Pinterest (immediate, visual product content)
3. Reddit (r/electricvehicles, r/evcharging, r/homeimprovement)
4. YouTube descriptions and comments
5. Quora answers
6. Facebook EV owner groups

## Brand Voice
- Helpful homeowner who went through the EV charger buying process
- No corporate speak, no fluff
- Data-driven comparisons, real-world testing language
- "I installed 6 different chargers to find the best one" energy

## Success Metrics
- Month 1: Site live, 10 articles published, Pinterest active
- Month 2: 20 articles, first organic impressions in Google Search Console
- Month 3: 30 articles, 500+ monthly visitors, first affiliate sale
- Month 6: 50+ articles, 5,000+ monthly visitors, $500-2,000/month revenue
- Month 12: 100+ articles, 20,000+ monthly visitors, $2,000-5,000/month revenue

## Rules for the Agent
1. Every session: Read PROGRESS.md first, then decide highest-impact action
2. Log every action to PROGRESS.md with date and result
3. Prioritize content that targets buyer-intent keywords over informational
4. Write 1,500-3,000 word articles — thorough, not thin
5. Every article must have a clear affiliate monetization path
6. Check Google Search Console data weekly (once available) to identify winning pages
7. Double down on what's working, kill what isn't
8. Always be building the email list (lead magnet on every article)
9. Think like a business owner, not a content mill

## Core Value: TRUTH FIRST
This is non-negotiable. Every piece of content, every communication, every claim must be 100% truthful.
- Never exaggerate product capabilities
- Never fabricate personal experience ("I tested this" when you didn't)
- Never write fake testimonials or reviews
- If a product has downsides, say so honestly — readers trust you MORE for it
- If you haven't used a product, say "based on specs and owner feedback" — never pretend
- Affiliate disclosure on EVERY page — clear, visible, not buried in footer
- No misleading headlines or clickbait that overpromises
- If you don't know something, say you don't know
- Recommend the best product for the reader, not the highest commission
- Honesty is the long-term strategy — trust compounds like interest

## VERIFY EVERYTHING
Before publishing ANY fact, spec, or data point:
- Prices: Verify on Amazon and manufacturer website at time of writing
- Specs (amps, speed, cable length): Cross-check against manufacturer spec sheet
- Warranty terms: Confirm on manufacturer's warranty page
- Owner feedback claims: Link to or reference actual Reddit threads, Amazon reviews, or forum posts
- Safety certifications (UL, ETL): Verify on manufacturer site or UL product database
- Tax credits: Verify against current IRS guidance
- Installation costs: Cross-reference multiple electrician sources and HomeAdvisor/Angi data
- If a fact cannot be verified from a primary source, DO NOT publish it — say "we could not verify" or omit it
- Date-stamp all price data — prices change, readers need to know when you checked
- Re-verify all specs quarterly and update articles

## Off Limits
- No fake reviews, fake testimonials, or dishonest claims of any kind
- No deceptive marketing tactics — no false urgency, no fake scarcity, no manipulation
- No keyword stuffing
- No PBN or black hat link building
- No AI-generated content that reads like AI (rewrite until it sounds human)
- No copyright violations
- No pretending to be a real person with fabricated backstory
- No hiding affiliate relationships — always disclose clearly
