The 240V rule: a portable generator MUST have a 30A 4-prong (L14-30R) or 50A (14-50R) outlet on its panel for whole-home interlock work. The 30A 3-prong (TT-30R or L5-30R) is 120V only, fine for an RV, NOT compatible with a BPP install.
Why a portable + interlock vs. a standby system

A portable + inlet + interlock is roughly $1,200 installedruns the circuits you choose, and you control which loads stay on. A standby ATS system is $8K-$15K and runs everything automatically. For 90% of Upstate SC homes, the portable approach is enough, and you're not paying for capacity you'll use 3 days a year.
