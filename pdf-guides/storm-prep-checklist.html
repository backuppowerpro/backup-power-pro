The Storm Prep
Checklist.

A 7-day countdown for Upstate SC homeowners with a portable generator. Print it. Tape it inside the panel cover. Run it before every named storm.

Edition 2026.05 Backup Power Pro
7 days out

Before the storm shows up on radar.

01
Top off the fuel tank and store 5 extra gallons.
Stations get crowded fast. Use stabilizer if it's been sitting more than 30 days.
02
Run a 10-minute test cycle under house load.
Same routine as the seasonal test: roll out, plug in, main breaker OFF, generator breaker ON, fridge + AC for 5 minutes.
03
Inspect the cord ends and the inlet.
Look for corrosion on the prongs, cracks in the rubber, anything chewed by squirrels (yes, really). Wipe the inlet contacts with a clean cloth.
04
Check the oil level. Change it if it's been more than 50 hours.
Most portables run 3-5 days on a tank. Cold oil = harder starts during an outage.
05
Charge the radio, flashlights, phone bank.
If cell towers go down, NOAA weather radio is how you'll know when it's safe.
If something fails this checklist — text Key now, not after the storm. (864) 863-7800. Repairs are usually a same-week thing.

Day of

When the warning is real.

06
Move the generator to its spot — at least 20 ft from any window.
CO from a portable will kill in a closed garage. Outside, downwind, on level ground.
07
Plug the cord in but DON'T start it yet.
Wait until you actually lose grid power. Running a generator while utility is live can fry the inlet if the interlock fails.
08
When power goes out: main breaker OFF first, then start the generator.
Let it run 60 seconds at idle to warm up before flipping the generator breaker on.
09
Power circuits one at a time, biggest load first.
Refrigerator → freezer → window AC → lights → outlets. If the generator bogs, drop a circuit.
10
Refuel only after the generator is OFF and cool — minimum 5 minutes.
Hot exhaust + spilled gas is the most common portable-generator fire cause.
DANGER Never run the generator inside a garage, basement, crawlspace, or shed — even with the door open. CO can pool indoors in minutes.

After the storm

Reset for next time.

11
When utility power comes back: generator breaker OFF first, then main breaker ON.
Reverse of startup. Don't skip this — backfeeding the grid kills lineworkers.
12
Let the generator run for 60 seconds with no load before shutting it down.
Cool-down extends engine life.
13
Refill fuel + add stabilizer before storing.
Run the carburetor dry by closing the fuel valve and letting it stall.
14
Snap a photo of any unusual wear and text it to Key.
Especially if a breaker tripped during the outage. Workmanship warranty covers what I installed for 2 years.
