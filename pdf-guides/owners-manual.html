When the power goes out

Your 5-step outage protocol.

01
Confirm the outage isn't just your house.
Check Duke Energy outage map (or call 800-769-3766). If it's a tripped main breaker on your end, the generator won't help.
02
Roll the generator outside, minimum 20 ft from any window.
Never run it inside a garage, even with the door open. CO will pool and kill in minutes. Downwind, level ground, dry surface.
03
Plug the yellow L14-30 cord into the inlet, THEN start the generator.
Hot before plug = arc damage to the prongs. Plug first, start second. Let it idle 60 seconds to warm up.
04
Inside the panel: main breaker OFF first, then the generator breaker ON.
The interlock plate enforces this sequence physically. If it won't slide, you're trying to do them in the wrong order — that's by design.
05
Flip on circuits one at a time, biggest load first.
Refrigerator → freezer → AC → lights → outlets. If the generator bogs (RPMs drop, lights dim), drop a circuit. Don't flip them all at once.
Reverse order to shut down: generator breaker OFF → main breaker ON → unplug cord → let generator run 60 seconds with no load → shut off.

Maintenance schedule

Five things that double its lifespan.

CadenceAction
Every month Visual check: cover the inlet, look for nests/dust/water staining inside the box.
Every 3 months Run a 10-minute test cycle under house load. Same routine as outage protocol, steps 02–05.
Twice a year (Jun + Nov) Pre-storm-season check. Run for 30 min on actual house load, top off oil, inspect cord ends.
Every 50 hours Change the oil. Most portables ship with break-in oil; first change is at 25 hours, then every 50.
Every 100 hours Air filter check, spark plug check, fuel filter (if equipped).
Annually Fuel system clean-out: drain old fuel, refill with stabilized fresh, run 5 min to circulate.
The single most common failure — old gas. Generators that sit 6+ months on un-stabilized fuel will gum up the carb. Fix is a $30 carb cleaner kit and 2 hours, OR add Sta-Bil to the tank twice a year.
Troubleshooting · the 4 most common
SymptomWhat to check first
Won't startFuel valve open? Choke set? Oil sensor (most portables won't start with low oil).
Starts then diesOld gas / clogged carburetor. Rare: vacuum lock in the gas tank cap.
Bogs under loadToo many circuits at once. Drop the AC or fridge first, restart sequence.
Trips main when connectingMain breaker should be OFF before generator breaker ON. Reverse the order.
