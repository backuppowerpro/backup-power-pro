While We're in Your Panel

Most Homes Have Zero Surge Protection

Power strips protect one outlet. A whole-home surge protector installs at your breaker panel and protects everything — your HVAC, refrigerator, washer, dryer, TVs, and anything else plugged in. It catches voltage spikes from lightning, utility switching, and the everyday surges you never notice that quietly shorten the life of your electronics.

When power comes back on after an outage, the initial surge is one of the most damaging moments for your home's electrical system. Since we're already in your panel for the inlet, adding surge protection takes minimal extra time.

We'll cover whether it makes sense for your home on the video call.

Power Strip
  • Protects one outlet
  • Wears out silently over time
  • No protection for hardwired appliances
  • Can actually cause fires when degraded
Whole-Home Surge Protector
  • Protects every circuit in your panel
  • Covers HVAC, fridge, washer, dryer
  • Guards against lightning and utility surges
  • Installed once, protects everything
