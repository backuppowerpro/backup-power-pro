## Curated Generator List by Category

### Category 1 — Budget Gas ($300-700): Best value for lights + fridge during outage

**Winner: Champion Power Equipment 3500W Dual Fuel Inverter (model ~200977 / 4250W variant)**
- Running/Peak: 3,500W running / 4,250W starting (gas); 3,150W / 3,825W (propane)
- Fuel: Dual fuel (gas or propane)
- Price: ~$500-650 on Amazon
- Key selling points: Dual fuel flexibility (propane stores longer = better for prep), inverter-clean power for electronics, quieter than open-frame, CO Shield safety shutoff
- Amazon rating: 4.3+ stars, 1,000+ reviews
- Why it wins: Dual fuel at this price point is rare. Propane stores indefinitely — perfect for homeowners who don't want stale gas. Inverter clean power means safe for laptops, phones, medical devices. This is the "all-rounder" budget pick.
- Affiliate program: Amazon Associates (3%)
- **BPP relevance: HIGH** — this wattage runs lights, fridge, phone chargers, and a window AC on 5,000W circuits. Enough to justify a $1,197 inlet installation.

**Runner-up: DuroMax XP5500EH**
- Running/Peak: 4,500W / 5,500W
- Fuel: Dual fuel (gas or propane)
- Price: ~$700-750 on Amazon
- Specs: Electric start, 8.8/10 rating, 12,000+ reviews
- Why mention it: More wattage for similar price. Open-frame (louder), but runs more appliances.

### Category 2 — Mid-Range Inverter ($700-1,500): Best dual-fuel/inverter for whole-home essentials

**Winner: Westinghouse iGen4500DF**
- Running/Peak: 3,700W running / 4,500W starting
- Fuel: Dual fuel (gas or propane), inverter
- Price: ~$800-1,000 on Amazon
- Key selling points: 52 dB (extremely quiet), 18-hour runtime at 25% load, RV-ready 30A outlet, remote electric start, clean power (<3% THD), parallel capable
- Rating: 4.4+ stars
- Why it wins: The 4,500W peak handles central air units (3-3.5 ton) on startup. The 18-hour runtime on one tank at partial load is exceptional. Remote start + quiet operation = "set and forget" during outages. Dual fuel = flexibility.
- Affiliate program: Amazon Associates (3%) — ~$24-30/sale
- **BPP relevance: VERY HIGH** — this is the sweet spot wattage that a BPP inlet box unlock. 3,700W running watts powers essential circuits in most SC homes.

**Runner-up: Honda EU2200i**
- Running/Peak: 1,800W / 2,200W
- Price: ~$1,100-1,200 on Amazon
- Why mention: Gold standard for reliability and quietness (48-57 dB). Only 2,200W limits whole-home use, but this is THE trust-builder for price-sensitive premium buyers who want Honda reliability.
- Note: Lower wattage means fewer circuits — relevant for BPP pitch (they need the right generator to justify the inlet install).

### Category 3 — Premium Quiet/Inverter ($1,500-3,000)

**Winner: Honda EU3000iS**
- Running/Peak: 2,800W / 3,000W
- Fuel: Gas, inverter
- Price: ~$2,400-2,800 on Amazon/authorized dealers
- Key selling points: 57-65 dB (quietest in class), electric start, fuel injection (no carb issues), 20 hours at 1/4 load, 7-year warranty with Honda dealer support, parallel capable to 6,000W
- Rating: 4.7+ stars, legendary reliability
- Why it wins: For buyers who want a generator that outlasts their house, this is the answer. Honda's direct-injection engine eliminates carb maintenance. 7-year warranty is the longest in portable generators. The high price self-justifies to buyers comparing it to a $15K standby — which connects directly to BPP's anchor.
- Affiliate: Amazon Associates (3%) — ~$72-84/sale
- **BPP relevance: VERY HIGH** — someone buying a $2,500 Honda generator is absolutely the profile who will spend $1,197 on a proper inlet install. They are NOT the extension cord type.

**Runner-up: Generac GP3500iO (iQ3500)**
- Running/Peak: 3,000W / 3,500W
- Price: ~$1,000-1,200 on HD/Amazon
- Why mention: The Honda alternative for buyers who balk at the price. Quieter than most in class, Generac brand recognition (people know standby = Generac), electric start. Better wattage for the money than Honda EU3000.

### Category 4 — Solar/Battery Power Station ($500-2,000)

**Winner: Jackery Explorer 2000 v2**
- Capacity: 2,042 Wh, 2,200W AC output
- Technology: LiFePO4 (longer lifespan, safer)
- Price: ~$749-999 on Amazon (frequently on sale)
- Key selling points: LiFePO4 battery = 3,000+ charge cycles (10+ years), 2,200W pure sine wave AC, USB-C PD 100W, solar-chainable to 1,200W input, no fumes (safe indoors), quiet
- Rating: 4.5+ stars
- Why it wins: The 2026 pricing (seen at $749 during Amazon sale in March 2026) makes this absurdly competitive. LiFePO4 over lithium-ion is a meaningful safety and longevity upgrade. 2,000Wh runs a fridge 15-20 hours.
- Affiliate: Jackery direct (5-8%) — ~$37-60/sale at $749 price; ~$50-80 at $999
- **BPP relevance: LOW** — solar/battery stations do NOT connect to home panel circuits. They run appliances directly. An inlet box is NOT relevant for this category. Do NOT cross-sell BPP installation here. Instead, use this category to capture solar-curious buyers and warm them up.

**Runner-up: Bluetti AC200Max**
- Capacity: 2,048 Wh, 2,200W AC output, expandable to 8,192 Wh with B230/B300 add-on batteries
- Price: ~$1,299-1,499 on bluettipower.com
- Why mention: Expandability is unique — this is the only unit in this price range that grows with the user. Better for buyers who want a long-term ecosystem.
- Affiliate: Bluetti direct (5-10%) — ~$65-150/sale

### Category 5 — High Output ($1,500-3,500): Largest portable for HVAC or near whole-home

**Winner: Westinghouse WGen9500DF**
- Running/Peak: 9,500W / 12,500W
- Fuel: Dual fuel (gas or propane)
- Price: ~$900-1,200 on Amazon (frequently under $1,000)
- Key selling points: 12,500W peak handles central AC startup (which draws 4,000-6,000W surge), remote electric start, transfer-switch ready L14-30 outlet AND 50A outlet, 12-hour runtime, CO sensor, 3-year warranty
- Rating: 4.4+ stars
- Why it wins: The price-to-wattage ratio is unmatched in the high-output category. At under $1,000, you get 9,500 running watts — enough to run a whole 2,000 sq ft home. The 50A outlet is critical for whole-home transfer switch setups. Westinghouse is underrated brand recognition vs price.
- Affiliate: Amazon Associates (3%) — ~$27-36/sale at $900-1,200
- **BPP relevance: EXTREME** — this is THE generator that belongs on a BPP inlet box. 9,500W running watts justify every circuit in the panel. This product page should have the most aggressive BPP cross-sell CTA.

**Runner-up: Champion 9000W Open Frame Inverter (model 201067)**
- Running/Peak: 7,500W / 9,000W
- Price: ~$1,100-1,300 on Amazon
- Why mention: Inverter-clean power at 9,000W is unusual (most high-output are open-frame non-inverter). More expensive but cleaner electricity. Better for sensitive electronics.

| Feature | Why Expected | Complexity | Notes |
|---------|--------------|------------|-------|
| Comparison table for each category | Users expect side-by-side spec comparison before buying | Low | 3-5 columns: Price, Watts, Fuel, Key Feature, Rating |
| Affiliate "Buy Now" button | Primary monetization mechanism | Low | Amazon Associates link with tracking ID |
| Star rating display | Social proof; users expect to see aggregate rating | Low | Can pull from Amazon manually or use API |

| FAQ section per category | Captures long-tail "can this generator run my AC" searches | Medium | Also helps SEO |

## Anti-Features (Do Not Build)

| Anti-Feature | Why Avoid | What to Do Instead |
|--------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Reviewing standby/whole-home generators | Generac Guardian, Kohler, etc. are $5K-$20K products that compete with BPP's anchor price | Explicitly exclude. Mention them only to contrast ("You don't need to spend $15,000 — here's why"). |
| Reviewing commercial/industrial generators | Out of scope; different buyer persona | Hard exclude |
| Pricing grid that promises specific Amazon prices | Prices change and stale prices destroy trust | Always use "Check current price" button instead of a static price |
| Reviewing solar panels directly (separate from power stations) | Different category, different buyer, dilutes the site focus | Keep solar section to portable power stations only |
| Star rating widgets that require JS-heavy plugins | Page speed kills affiliate conversion | Use static HTML/CSS star representations or lightweight SVG |

## MVP Recommendation

Build in this order:

1. **Apply for Amazon Associates** (Day 1 — takes 1-3 days for approval)
2. **Homepage** with 5 category links and brief intro (Day 1-2)
3. **Westinghouse WGen9500DF page** — highest BPP cross-sell value, aggressive CTA (Day 2-3)
4. **Westinghouse iGen4500DF page** — mid-range sweet spot, most BPP-relevant customer profile (Day 3-4)
5. **Champion 4250W Dual Fuel page** — budget category, captures volume searchers (Day 4-5)
6. **Jackery Explorer 2000 v2 page** — apply to Jackery program simultaneously; highest per-sale commission (Day 5-6)
7. **Honda EU2200i / EU3000iS page** — premium category; trust-building content (Day 6-7)
8. **Westinghouse WGen9500DF already done above; add Champion 9000W as alternative** — high-output second option

- Bluetti page (apply to program simultaneously with Jackery; publish once approved)
- Wattage calculator widget (Phase 2 enhancement)
- Blog content / storm season posts (Phase 3)
- Local landing pages for Greenville/Spartanburg (Phase 4)
