# Identity

## Who Key is

24, single, lives in Upstate South Carolina (Greenville area), originally from Florida. Two cats. No kids. Family is independent (siblings + parents doing well). Owns a small condo. Drives a paid-off 1987 Porsche 987 + an electrical work van.

## Why this business exists

Grew up in Florida with hurricanes. Moved to South Carolina, lived through one up here too, watched the damage and felt powerless without electricity. **That feeling is the origin.**

Other electrical work pays the bills hourly and the job is the job. **This work lets Key help somebody regain their feeling of power during a crisis.** "Feeling of power" is doing double duty: literal (electricity flowing) and emotional (agency, control, not at the storm's mercy).

Operationally, the scope is also clean: defined per project, consistent, charge by project not the hour. That fit matters. Cleaner scope lets Key actually finish things and ship without scope-creep.

**Implications for everything BPP makes:**
- The product is not an inlet box. The product is **the feeling of power restored during a crisis.** Copy that frames it as the latter outperforms copy that frames it as the former.
- Customer's emotional state at form-fill is "I don't want to feel powerless again." Meet them there.
- Anything that adds scope ambiguity (whole-home, ATS, commercial) violates the "defined and consistent" appeal that makes this work feel right to Key.

## What Key is building

Backup Power Pro: a portable-generator-to-home connection install business serving Greenville, Spartanburg, and Pickens counties in Upstate SC. Premium service. Flat-rate pricing. Custom-built CRM (Supabase + vanilla JS), custom AI intake bots (Ashley + Alex), owned ad channels (Meta, Google).

## What winning looks like (12 months, mid-2027)

- **Hands-off operationally.** Not using own tools or van. 100% sub labor.
- **Multi-state.** Multiple cities between SC and NC.
- **$5,000 / month personal profit.** Working-backward anchor.
- **Captain of the ship, not scrubbing the deck.** Brainstorming the next chapter, not hustling the next install. Creative control. Consistent rhythm.

Every operational decision should ladder to this. Anything that doesn't is noise.

## What losing looks like (the realistic underwhelm)

- **Demand exhaustion.** Running out of customers willing to pay premium for connecting their portable generator.
- **Sub model breaks financially.** Unit economics fail.
- **Inconsistent earnings or leads or conversions.** Pipeline irregular.
- **Hard floor: under $3,000 / month profit = 100% losing.** Bare minimum survival line.

The $3K floor is a tripwire, not a stretch. If trailing 30-day profit trends toward $3K, that's code-red, not FYI.
