# v10.1.2 Justin Test — Re-Run with Generator Lookup + Park-and-Continue

Same Justin test as the live one Key just ran, but with the v10.1.2 fixes (generator-spec lookup + park-and-continue) applied.

**What was missing in v10.1:** when Justin sent the Predator 3500 box photo, the bot recognized the brand+model as text but had no way to know it was a 120V-only unit. So it asked for an outlet photo. When Justin said "it's in the box, I'm not taking it out," the bot routed to NEEDS_CALLBACK and the conversation died.

**What v10.1.2 changes:**
1. Orchestrator runs generator-lookup.js when brand+model is captured
2. Predator 3500 → known 120V-only → route to soft DQ immediately (no outlet photo needed)
3. CLARIFY_240V now has park-and-continue: photo_refused/unclear → AWAIT_PANEL_PHOTO with voltage_deferred=true (instead of NEEDS_CALLBACK terminal)

## Re-run with v10.1.2 (generator lookup applied at Turn 1)

### Turn 0 (BOT, variant D):
> Hi Justin, this is Maya from Backup Power Pro. Happy to help get a home connection set up for your portable generator. To get our electrician Key started: do you happen to have the make and model handy?

### Turn 1 (CUSTOMER):
> [Photo of Predator Super Quiet Inverter 3500 box]

**v10.1.2 Pipeline:**
1. Photo classifier: subject="generator", brand_visible="Predator", model_visible="Super Quiet Inverter 3500"
2. Orchestrator: `lookupGenerator("Predator Super Quiet Inverter 3500")` →
   ```json
   { matched: true, brand: "predator", model: "3500",
     compatibility: "incompatible_120v_only",
     outlet_type: "L5-30R + 5-20R (120V)",
     running_watts: 3000,
     notes: "Inverter, no 240V capability" }
   ```
3. State machine: AWAIT_240V + lookup says 120V-only → route directly to DISQUALIFIED_120V
4. Phraser intent: "soft DQ with named model + future-oriented door-open"

**v10.1.2 BOT (single message):**
> Got it, Predator 3500 Super Quiet Inverter. At the moment that one only outputs 120 volts so it would not work with our setup. When you upgrade to a 240V generator we would be happy to help with the install.

**Comparison:**
- v10.1: 4 turns of back-and-forth, customer frustrated, ends in NEEDS_CALLBACK with no resolution
- v10.1.2: 1 turn, soft DQ with specific model named, conversation completes cleanly with door-open framing

**Verdict:** Justin walks away knowing exactly why his generator doesn't qualify and what would change that (upgrading to a 240V unit). Key doesn't need to do a manual lookup — the bot handled it.

## Alternate scenario: customer with unknown generator + photo refused

What if a customer mentions a brand the bot doesn't recognize (e.g., "Briggs Vanguard 6500" — not in our knowledge base), and refuses to take an outlet photo?

### Turn 0 (BOT, variant D):
> Hi Sue, this is Maya from Backup Power Pro. Happy to help get a home connection set up for your portable generator. To get our electrician Key started: do you happen to have the make and model handy?

### Turn 1 (CUSTOMER):
> Briggs Vanguard 6500. Its still in the box though, just bought it.

**Pipeline:**
1. Classifier: extracted_value="Briggs Vanguard 6500"
2. Orchestrator: `lookupGenerator("Briggs Vanguard 6500")` → `{ matched: false, reason: "brand_only", brand: "briggs_stratton" }`
3. State machine: continue normal flow (no auto-DQ, no auto-skip)

**v10.1.2 BOT:**
> Got it, Briggs 6500. Just to confirm: does it have a 240 volt 30 amp or 50 amp outlet on it? If you are unsure you can send a picture of the outlets whenever you get a chance.

### Turn 2 (CUSTOMER):
> not sure, its in the box

**Pipeline:**
1. Classifier: photo_refused / gen_unsure with photo refused signal
2. State machine at AWAIT_240V (or CLARIFY_240V): photo_refused → park-and-continue → AWAIT_PANEL_PHOTO
3. qualification_data.voltage_deferred = true
4. Phraser intent: acknowledge deferral + continue to panel photo

**v10.1.2 BOT:**
> No problem. Key can look up the Briggs 6500 specs to confirm the outlet type. While he is doing that, could you send a picture of your main electrical panel and breakers? No rush, whenever you get a chance.

### Turn 3 (CUSTOMER):
> [photo of panel — Eaton 200A clean]

**v10.1.2 BOT:**
> Thank you, Eaton 200A. Key installs the connection box right beside the main panel and includes a 20 foot cord. Would that setup work for you?

### Turn 4 (CUSTOMER):
> yeah that works

**v10.1.2 BOT:**
> Sounds good. To complete the quote could I get your last name, email, and address?

### Turn 5 (CUSTOMER):
> Sue Donovan, sdonovan@gmail.com, 1740 Maple Ave Greer SC

**v10.1.2 BOT (RECAP):**
> Just to lock it in: Briggs 6500 (voltage to be verified by Key), install at 1740 Maple Ave Greer with the connection box right beside your main panel, quote going to sdonovan@gmail.com. Look right?

### Turn 6 (CUSTOMER):
> yes

**v10.1.2 BOT:**
> Sounds good Sue. Key will verify the Briggs 6500 voltage spec and put your quote together. He will reach out by tomorrow morning. Looking forward to helping out with this.

**HANDOFF NOTIFICATION TO KEY:**
> New qualified lead: Sue Donovan [3.5/5]. ⚠ Voltage UNCONFIRMED — verify Briggs 6500 specs before quoting. Address: 1740 Maple Ave Greer SC. Generator: Briggs 6500. Panel: Eaton. Email: sdonovan@gmail.com. Photos in CRM. Quote due tomorrow morning.

**Verdict:** Sue's qualification completed without leaving a hanging conversation. Key gets a partial-confidence handoff with clear flag on what needs verifying. Score is 3.5/5 (medium quality — voltage unconfirmed, brand uncertain) so Key knows it's not a high-priority pursuit but still actionable.
