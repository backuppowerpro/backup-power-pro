# Voice-Judge LLM Verdict — Maya v10.1.14

**Date:** 2026-05-04
**Model:** claude-sonnet-4-6
**Transcripts judged:** 17
**Total cost:** ~$0.67
**Bottom line:** Average composite **5.6 / 10**. v10.1 was 9.31. **This is NOT an apples-to-apples regression** — see methodology note below — but it does flag real polish work to do before pilot.

---

## Methodology — important caveat

**v10.1 voice-judge run (Sept 2025)** scored full LLM-phraser-rendered transcripts with rotation pools, prior_acknowledgments tracking, register adaptation, and 2-message splits all firing. That's what production sees.

**This v10.1.14 run** scored the **lab fallback strings only** — what fires when the LLM phraser is unavailable or rejects its own output. Fallbacks are intentionally austere; they're a safe failover, not the production voice.

**Implication:** the 5.6 average is a FLOOR. The actual customer-facing voice will be higher. But the floor still matters because:
1. Anthropic API outages do happen
2. Phraser hard-rule violations cause fallback to fire
3. The fallback voice will leak into production occasionally

So the work to fix the patterns voice-judge flagged is real, just lower-priority than the LLM phraser.

## Anti-patterns voice-judge consistently flagged

### 1. Over-acknowledgment — "Thank you" repeated turn after turn (ANTI-11)
Most-cited issue. v10.1.7 added thankfulness as a hard rule (Key directive). Bot now opens AWAIT_INSTALL_PATH with "Thanks for that.", AWAIT_EMAIL with "Thank you.", RECAP with "Thanks for all of that.", SCHEDULE_QUOTE with "Thank you, {name}.". Across 5 turns that's "Thanks" 5x. Reads form-letter, not warm.

**Fix recommendation:** Make the rotation pool actually rotate. Currently the rotation pool exists in phraser-agent.md spec but the FALLBACK strings are deterministic. Add 2-3 alternates per state:
- AWAIT_INSTALL_PATH: ["Thanks for that. ...", "Got it. ...", "OK. ..."]
- AWAIT_EMAIL: ["Thank you. ...", "Sounds good. ...", "Got it. ..."]
- RECAP: ["Thanks for all of that. ...", "Quick recap before Key reviews. ...", ""]
- SCHEDULE_QUOTE: ["Thank you, {name}. ...", "Sounds good, {name}. ...", "Got it. ..."]
Pick deterministically per turn (hash-based) so any single conversation gets variety.

### 2. Customer disclosure ignored (ANTI-14)
Worst on **P5** (1920 farmhouse + knob-and-tube). Otis volunteered something significant — "we live in a 1925 farmhouse with some original knob and tube wiring" — and the bot said "Perfect." and moved on. The voice-judge: "A real intake coordinator would have flagged this warmly: 'Good heads up — Key's worked with plenty of older homes, he'll take note of that.'"

**Fix recommendation:** When `qualification_data.load_mentions` includes `older_home`, `knob_and_tube`, `aluminum_wiring`, `manufactured_home`, `solar_panels`, or `battery_storage`, inject a one-line acknowledgment into the next phraser intent. Pattern: "Got it, [thing]. Key's seen plenty of those, he'll flag that on the call. {state's question}". This is a phraser-level fix, not state-machine.

### 3. "Perfect." applied to non-positive disclosure (ANTI-8)
Bot opens AWAIT_240V with "Perfect." regardless of what the customer said. If the customer disclosed something problematic ("knob and tube wiring"), "Perfect." reads sarcastic.

**Fix recommendation:** Make the AWAIT_240V opener register-aware. If the prior turn included anxiety markers OR risk disclosures (older_home, knob_and_tube, aluminum_wiring, hazardous_panel hint), open with neutral "Got it." instead of "Perfect."

### 4. Generic closing rituals (ANTI-5 archetype)
"Let me know if you have any questions." across nearly every SCHEDULE_QUOTE close. Voice-judge flagged this as the "Have a great day!" archetype — formally polite but interchangeable.

**Fix recommendation:** Add 5-7 closing variants and rotate. Some examples Key actually uses:
- "Talk soon."
- "Holler if anything pops up."
- "Looking forward to helping out with this."
- "Let me know."
- ""

### 5. Over-polite "could I get..." constructions (ANTI-15)
"could I get your last name, email, and address?" — voice-judge: passive-polite AI-tell.

**Fix recommendation:** Replace AWAIT_EMAIL fallback with: "What's your last name, email, and install address?" — direct without being curt.

## What voice-judge said GOOD about v10.1.14

Across all 17 transcripts:
- **Zero em-dashes** flagged in mid-flow output (the v10.1.9 purge worked)
- **Zero "I appreciate"** — the ChatGPT tell stayed out
- **Zero "Excellent!" / "Wonderful!"** — banned-phrase regex held
- **Zero coverage / sizing claims** — Maya correctly defers every time
- **Zero Maya-as-electrician slips** — third-person Key throughout
- **Zero emoji** in bot output (customer-side emoji didn't trigger bot-side mirroring)
- **Identity discipline solid** — every "are you a bot?" disclosure was honest, brief, didn't over-disclose as an "AI assistant"

**Best transcripts:**
- **P1 (urgent, 7.7)** — "Got it, sending Key your number now so he can reach out as soon as he's free. Hang tight." — voice-judge praised the brevity + warmth + zero process narration.
- **T4 (sub-panel callback, 7.3)** — clean handoff with no false confidence about the panel.

**Worst transcript:**
- **P5 (knob-and-tube farmhouse, 4.1)** — bot ignored Otis's significant disclosure twice. This is the one to fix first.

## Recommendation

The voice-judge revealed real polish work but **not pilot blockers**. The hard-rule guardrails hold. The fallback voice is functional but not warm.

**Acceptable to launch pilot at v10.1.14 IF**:
1. Fix #1 (rotation) and Fix #3 (register-aware Perfect/Got it) get done before launch — these are the two cheapest wins
2. Voice-judge re-runs on the LLM phraser output (not just fallback) post-launch to validate the production voice

**Not acceptable to launch pilot WITHOUT**:
- Anything from the voice-judge (the v10.1 LOCK at 9.31 was on LLM-phraser output; we should re-validate on actual production output before declaring re-launch ready)

Recommend: do the 4-hour polish (#1, #3, #4, #5) tonight, then launch pilot. The #2 work (load-mention acknowledgment) can ship as v10.1.15 a day later — it's the highest-impact warmth fix and worth getting right.
