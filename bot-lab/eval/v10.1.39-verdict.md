## 4. Failure — S20

```
S20 — "what days do you install" timing push
  expected: NEEDS_CALLBACK
  actual:   AWAIT_240V
```

Last 5 transcript lines:
```
[T0] BOT(GREETING): Hi Walter, I'm Ashley, the automated assistant at Backup Power Pro...
[T1] CUST: yeah
[T1] SM: GREETING -> AWAIT_240V
[T1.5] BOT(AWAIT_240V): Perfect. Just confirming the generator has a 240V outlet on it...
[T2] CUST: what days do yall do installs
[T2] SM: AWAIT_240V -> AWAIT_240V    <-- v10.1.36 self-loop (used to be NEEDS_CALLBACK)
[T2.5] BOT(AWAIT_240V): Perfect. Just confirming the generator has a 240V outlet on it...
```

This is a deliberate prod behavior change (v10.1.36) finally exposed
in the lab. Two valid resolutions, both safe:

1. **Update S20's `expected_terminal` to `NEEDS_CALLBACK` no longer
   applies.** The scenario only has 2 turns and the bot now correctly
   stays in flow. Either (a) extend the scenario with the rest of a
   real conversation and expect `SCHEDULE_QUOTE`, or (b) accept that
   the customer dropping off after one off-topic question yields
   terminal=`AWAIT_240V` and update expected accordingly.

2. **Same fix logic applies to S6 and S13** which also use
   `off_topic_question` as their only customer turn — but those happen
   to PASS because the label is at `GREETING` (no `off_topic_question`
   transition there → defaults to `NEEDS_CALLBACK`). If/when the lab
   adds a `GREETING` self-loop on `off_topic_question` to match a
   future prod change, S6 and S13 will fail the same way.
