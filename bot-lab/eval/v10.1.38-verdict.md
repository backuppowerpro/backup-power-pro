## 3. Top 3 tuning targets

1. **Port lab JS forward to v10.1.32–v10.1.36 transitions.** The 8 deliberate prod-only changes above mean the eval is silently behind. Highest-leverage fix: copy the `off_topic_question` self-loops + `friendly_chitchat → SCHEDULE_QUOTE/COMPLETE` lines into `bot-lab/state-machine.js`, then add 4–5 new scenarios that EXERCISE those paths ("do you do Generac?", "are you licensed?", "thanks great") so future drift in those rules gets caught. Without this, the next regression in the off-topic-question handler ships unnoticed.

2. **Add an explicit `expected_terminal` field to every scenario.** Right now expected terminals are only inferable from scenario names + a separate baseline transcript. Every future evaluator (human or agent) has to re-derive them. One-line addition per scenario, locks the contract permanently, kills the "is this a regression or a baseline shift?" ambiguity that this run nearly tripped on.

3. **Add a sub-panel-only-on-second-photo scenario for `CONFIRM_MAIN_BREAKER` ambiguity.** The current main-breaker-unsure logic only has T3 (clear ambiguity → ask) and T4 (clearly sub-panel → callback). Missing: customer sends ambiguous panel, says "yes there's a main 200A breaker at the top" but follow-up photo contradicts. This is the realistic Tyler-round-3 case and `CONFIRM_MAIN_BREAKER` self-loop behavior on disagreement isn't covered. Costs nothing, prevents a class of "Maya accepted the customer's word over the photo" handoffs.
